Ingredients

  • One large pork tenderloin
  • 2 cups bourbon
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup salt
  • 6 squished garlic cloves
  • 2 pulled rosemary sprigs
  • 1 clump of of thyme pulled
  • 1/8 cup fresh ground black pepper
  • 1/2 zest of tangerine (or favorite citrus)
  • 6 pieces chopped fresh ginger (about the size of a garlic clove)
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 1 large tablespoon of local honey
  • 1 large shallot
  • Apricot preserve
  • 2 tablespoons of butter

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 425 dgerees farenheit
  2. Pull the pork tenderloin from the brine after being fully submerged for at least 24 hour and set aside on rack
  3. Remove the garlic cloves, rosemary, thyme, and ginger from the brine and evenly distribute the ingredients in the shape and size of the tenderloin on a cookie sheet lined with parchment paper. You are creating a bed for the tenderloin to rest upon.
  4. Julienne a large shallot and add it to the tenderloin's bed
  5. Heat up your favorite pan on the stovetop with medium-high heat
  6. While your pan is heating lightly salt and pepper all sides of the tenderloin
  7. Put some high heat oil in the pan, I like to use rendered duck fat, and once the oil is hot sear all sides of the tenderloin. You are not trying to cook it. You are looking for a light crust and some color; about 1-2 minutes per "side". Once all sides have seared place the tenderloin on its bed.
  8. Put a small saucepan on the stovetop over medium low heat. Drop 4 large tablespoons of apricot preserve, the 2 tablespoons of butter and a shot of bourbon into the the saucepan stirring often. Add a pinch of salt, black pepper, and freshly ground nutmeg. Combine and bring to a light simmer for 4 minutes or so stirring frequently. Once thickened brush the tnederloin with the apricot bourbon glaze liberally.
  9. Place the tenderloin baking sheet in the oven and bake at 425 degrees farenheit for ~30 minutes (Aiming for an internalk temperature of 145 degrees farenheit.
