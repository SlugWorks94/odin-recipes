Smashburger Recipe

What makes a good smashburger? A smaller more manageable, not too messy to eat burger with a patty that has a nice crust. The trick is in the The A cast iron griddle preheated medium-high to high heat, a half tablespoon of butter, and an ambient temperature 1/4 pound of mince formed into a ball and immediately smashed as soon as it hits the hot griddle.

Smashburgers are very easy to make. To make a good smashburger all the effort is in the prep and ingredients. They cook extremely fast so if you want a nice hot one fresh off the griddle, it is best to have all your assebly ingredients layed out on the counter for a speedy assembly.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of mince formed into 1/4 pound "meat balls" (makes 4 burgers)
  • 4 small burger buns, the lighter the better
  • 4 slices of american cheese
  • An heirloom tomato around the same diameter as the buns, sliced thin
  • Butter lettuce or clasic iceberg lettuce, full leaves or chopped
  • Thinly sliced white onion
  • Thousand island or the like dressing

Steps

  1. Preheat cast iron griddle over medium-high to high heat
  2. Preheat oven to 400 degrees
  3. Have all ingredients layed out and ready for assembly
  4. Take the half talbespoon of butter and place it on the hot spot of the griddle
  5. Place the "meat ball" in the pool of butter and smash it into a thin patty with either a spatula or a steak weight. First side should be done within 3 minutes
  6. Put the bun in the preheated oven while the first side of your patty is cooking
  7. Flip the patty after the 3ish minutes are up, there should be a nice crust, place the american cheese on top of the patty, cover with a stainless bowl to help melt the cheese
  8. Pull the bun from the oven and spread your dressing of choice on both halves
  9. Place your lettuce in the bottom half of the bun to act as a bed for your patty (It will help keep your bun from getting too soggy)
  10. Pull the patty from the griddle and place on top of the lettuce
  11. Then layer a slice of tomato on top of the patty followed by a thin slices of raw white onion
